The entire worldview is composed of multiple worlds. These worlds are not completely isolated from one another, but neither are they parallel spaces that can be freely entered at will. Each world has its own stable laws, social order, and logic of identity. For individuals living within them, crossing between worlds is not merely a change of location; it also means facing different boundaries of power, social identities, and rules of action.

The main stages are concentrated in the following worlds:
- **The three-dimensional world M3.** This is the real world, where \wkn{} exists as a human. There are no publicly acknowledged special abilities, miracles, or two-dimensional laws here; everything operates according to the logic of real society.
- **A certain two-dimensional world M2.** This is a modern beastman world. At the level of social cognition and public belief, there is no recognized god here; however, at the ontological level of the World, it is still governed by \wkn{} as its Creator. Special abilities exist here. The world preserves the structure of modern society while also accommodating racial differences, differences in ability, and complex mobility of identity.
- **The Wolf World WF.** A world controlled by \wf{}, where special abilities exist. This world takes thunder, discipline, loyalty, and collective responsibility as its core character, and possesses a strong sense of order.
- **The Dragon World DR.** A world controlled by \dr{}, where special abilities exist. This world takes fire, creation, etiquette, talent, and glory as its core character, combining a sense of civilization with competitiveness.

The worlds M2, WF, and DR follow all laws described in Chapter 1.
